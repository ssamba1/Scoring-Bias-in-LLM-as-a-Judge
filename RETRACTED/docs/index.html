Scoring Bias in LLM-as-a-Judge Models

A 22-Model Landscape with Base-Instruct Comparison

Sricharan Samba · South Forsyth High School · arXiv:2607.xxxxx · July 2026

31 Model Variants 54,000+ Judgments 3 Bias Probes 9 Base-Instruct Pairs

Abstract

Background: LLMs deployed as automated judges exhibit systematic scoring biases, but whether these originate from pre-training or instruction tuning is unknown.

Methods: We measure scoring bias across 22 instruct-tuned models (29,700 judgments) and compare base vs instruct variants across 9 families (24,300 judgments) using 3 perturbation probes.

Findings: Score ID bias has the largest average effect (Δ = 0.68) across instruct models. Instruction tuning reduces format bias but may increase content bias for RLHF families. The single SFT+DPO family shows a different pattern.

Conclusions: Scoring bias is modulated by instruction tuning, not inherent to pre-training.

Key Findings

📊

Score ID Bias is Largest

Δ = 0.68

Score ID (label format) shows the largest average effect across all 22 instruct models. Numeric labels are recommended for scoring rubrics.

⬇️

Format Bias Decreases

−44% to −77%

Instruction tuning reduces format-related bias (rubric order, score ID) across 7 of 9 model families. Format parsing becomes more efficient.

📈

Content Bias Scale-Dependent

+35% (3B+)

Content bias increases after instruction tuning in larger (3B+) RLHF models but decreases in smaller models (≤1.5B). This is the central finding.

🔬

Format Efficiency Hypothesis

23.7% → 20.8%

Format token attention decreases after instruction tuning, suggesting more efficient parsing rather than attention redistribution as the mechanism.

🧪

Training Method Matters

7 RLHF vs 1 SFT+DPO

Seven RLHF families show the differential effect. The SFT+DPO family (Mistral 7B) and SFT-only family (StableLM 2) show different patterns.

🧮

Bayes Factor > 10,000

Overwhelming Evidence

All three probes show BF₁₀ > 10,000 across the 22-model landscape, confirming that scoring bias exists across instruct-tuned models.
