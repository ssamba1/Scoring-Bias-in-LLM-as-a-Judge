How it works: Each judge's score is adjusted by its Interaction Ratio (IR). Judges with high IR (compounding) have their scores discounted to reflect that their evaluations are more affected by bias. The corrected score is: corrected = raw × (1 / IR). A score of 3.50 from Claude (IR=1.72) becomes 2.03 after correction.
