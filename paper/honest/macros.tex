\newcommand{\PATCHPROSE}{We test causality directly on Qwen2.5-1.5B (28 layers; rubric-order bias; $n=35$ items with a non-trivial base--instruct score gap). Patching the instruct model's score-position residual into the base model is \emph{inert} at early layers (fraction of items moved toward the instruct score $\approx 0$ for layers 0--9), then jumps in a single layer---from $0.06$ at layer~9 to $0.89$ at layer~10---and reaches $100\%$ from layer~14 onward (Fig.~\ref{fig:patch}). The correction is therefore not diffuse but computed in a sharp, phase-transition-like band around layer~10: before it the base representation carries no fix, and after it the transplanted instruct representation \emph{fully} determines the score. This is an intervention, not a correlation, and confirms P3; it also localizes \emph{where} instruction tuning writes the bias-relevant computation.}

\newcommand{\GOLDPROSE}{With no perturbation, both base and instruct judges discriminate almost perfectly (accuracy $0.98$; Table~\ref{tab:gold}), and instruct judges are the more confident, with a larger good$-$bad margin ($1.31$ vs $0.82$). Nuisances then do real damage: rubric reversal collapses accuracy to chance for both ($0.02$ base, $0.00$ instruct), and verbosity padding costs about 30 points ($0.68$/$0.70$); authority framing is nearly harmless ($0.95$/$0.97$). Instruction tuning is \emph{not} protective---its margin loss equals or exceeds the base model's (reversed $2.53$ vs $1.46$; verbose $1.00$ vs $0.78$). This is exactly what the mechanism predicts: a more decisive judge commits harder, which widens the good--bad margin but does not immunize it against a perturbation that displaces the whole score distribution. Bias is not cosmetic---it corrupts real quality judgments (P6).}

\newcommand{\DISCUSSION}{The audit is cheap because it needs no perturbations, no labels, and no second model---just the entropy of one scoring call.}
\newcommand{\CONCLUSION}{Across five distinct bias families and \NFAM{} model families, LLM-as-a-judge bias is governed by a single quantity: the decisiveness of the judge's score distribution. The account is theoretical (a variance bound on nuisance-sensitivity), measured (entropy predicts bias), causal (activation patching localizes the fix to a mid-network band), and predictive (bias estimated out-of-sample from one unperturbed forward pass). It reframes debiasing from enumerating symptoms to shaping one distribution; it explains, via a decisiveness/responsiveness decomposition, why instruction tuning attenuates some biases and amplifies others; and it yields a deployable, label-free bias audit. We release a single-script reproduction and, because a mechanism is only as trustworthy as the measurement beneath it---as our own parse-failure confound shows---a full integrity audit of a prior, unreliable version of this project.}
