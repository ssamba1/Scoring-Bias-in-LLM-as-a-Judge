## Part 1: How Hard Is Each Venue?

| Venue | Difficulty | Acceptance Rate | What It Takes |
|-------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **arXiv** | **Trivial** | 100% | 30 min, any legitimate paper. No review. |
| **Zenodo** | **Trivial** | 100% | Connect GitHub repo, click a button. |
| **ISEF (affiliated fair)** | **Moderate** | ~30% to finals | Win your regional fair first. ~1,300 finalists globally. |
| **ISEF Grand Award** | **Hard** | ~2% | Top ~200 of 1,300 finalists win cash prizes. |
| **NeurIPS HS Track** | **Moderate** | ~25% acceptance | 4-page paper with real results. Novel contribution. |
| **ACL Findings** | **Moderate** | ~30% | 8-page paper, solid experiments. |
| **ACL Main Conference** | **Hard** | ~20% | Same as Findings but requires stronger significance. |
| **Nature/Science** | **Extremely hard** | <8% | Not realistic for this work. |

**Our realistic targets (ordered):**
1. **arXiv** — Guaranteed. Do today.
2. **ISEF** — Achievable. 83/100 estimated. Need to present well.
3. **NeurIPS HS Track** — Achievable. Our paper matches accepted papers.
4. **ACL Findings** — Achievable with the 44-family data. Would need to add human eval.

## Part 2: Should You Try Multiple Things?

**Yes, absolutely.** They are not mutually exclusive. Here's the order:

| Step | What | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| **Step 1** | **arXiv now** | Establishes priority. Gives you a citable paper. |
| **Step 2** | **ISEF in parallel** | Different audience. Judges care about presentation, not publication status. |
| **Step 3** | **NeurIPS HS Track** | After arXiv + ISEF. Builds on both. |
| **Step 4** | **ACL Findings** | If NeurIPS rejects, submit here. The data doesn't change. |

**You can have all four simultaneously.** An arXiv preprint doesn't prevent you from submitting to conferences (most conferences explicitly allow arXiv preprints). ISEF doesn't care about arXiv.

## Part 3: How This Looks for College

**This is a strong profile item.** Here's where it ranks:

| Achievement | Impact on Applications | Context |
|------------|----------------------|---------|
| **arXiv paper** | ✅ **Positive** | Shows research capability. Many high school applicants don't have this. |
| **ISEF finalist** | ✅ **Strong positive** | Top ~1,300 globally. Elite programs notice this. |
| **ISEF Grand Award** | ✅✅ **Very strong** | Top ~200 globally. Opens doors at MIT, Stanford, Caltech. |
| **NeurIPS publication** | ✅✅✅ **Exceptional** | Extremely rare for high school students. Would be a standout factor. |
| **ACL publication** | ✅✅✅ **Exceptional** | Same as NeurIPS. Rare for high school. |

**How to frame it in your application:**

> "I designed and conducted the first base-vs-instruct comparison of scoring bias in LLM-as-a-Judge, testing 44 model families (88 variants) across 3 bias probes totaling 118,800 judgments on $0 compute budget. My findings revealed a novel differential effect — instruction tuning improves format robustness but increases content sensitivity — answering an open question from published literature (Li et al., DASFAA 2026). I published this work on arXiv and presented it at ISEF."

That paragraph alone signals: research ability, independent work, resourcefulness, novel contribution, publication record.
